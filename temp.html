Latitude vs. Temperature

When you examine the plot of latitude versus temperature it makes sense that temperature increases as a city gets closer to the equator. What was interesting was the lack of symmetry about the equator. Two factors could have lead to this lack of symmetry. First, the time of year the data was collected, summer in the northern hemisphere, leads the hotter temperatures to be north of the equator. Also, not having an equal number of cities on either side of the equator could be a flaw in the collection of the data.
